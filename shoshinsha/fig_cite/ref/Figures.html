Figures & Tables

Figures

Rule:

Fig. #. Caption of figure. (x) Caption of part (if applicable). (x) Caption of part (if applicable). [#]

Example:

Fig. 4. Intercomplex crosstalk characteristics. (a) Electrode transmission. (b) Interelectrode crosstalk. [1]
Guidelines:

Always place the Figure title/caption below the figure

The Figure, and Figure title/captions must be centre-justified

When referring to multiple parts of figures: Use the singular Fig., not Figs. (e.g. Fig. 4(a) and 4(b)).

If you adapt, amend, or otherwise modify the figure, indicate this at the end of your caption

Still unsure? Refer to the IEEE Editorial Style manual for Authors

 

Images

Note: To reference images, follow the guidelines for Figures (above), including only the relevant/available information.

Rule:

Fig. #. Caption of image. Adapted from [#]

Example:

Fig. 8. 5G Wireless tower. Adapted from [2]
Guidelines:

Always place the Image title/caption below the image

If you adapt, amend, or otherwise modify the image, indicate this at the end of your caption

Still unsure? Refer to the IEEE Editorial Style manual for Authors

 

Tables

Rule:

TABLE # (in Roman numerals)
Text of Caption. (a) Text of Part A Caption. (b) Text of Part B Caption [#]

Example:

TABLE IX
Optimal Wavelength as a Function of Polarizer Angle. (a) Wavelength for External Cavity. (b) Estimated Wavelength for Laser Diode [2]
Guidelines:

Always place the table title/caption above the table

The Table, and Table title/captions must be centre-justified

​When referring to multiple parts of tables: Use the singular Table, not Tables. (e.g. Table. IX(a) and IX(b)).

​If you adapt, amend, or otherwise modify the table, indicate this at the end of your caption

Still unsure? Refer to the IEEE Editorial Style manual for Authors
